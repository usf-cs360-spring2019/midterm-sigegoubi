Heatmap Comparison between Rich Neighborhoods and Poor Neighborhoods on Response Time


Caption

The above chart shows the median parent household income (adjusted for inflation) versus mobility rate, calculated as the percent of students with parents in the bottom 20% of income distribution that reach the top 20% in income distribution. Circles are sized by average number of students per cohort, and colored by median child individual earnings in 2014 (rounded to the nearest $100). According to the average living cost of a single person in California, mid-point of the color legend represent the amount of $55,000.

Interpretation

In this bubble chart, the only difference with in-class example is it colored by "Median Child Earnings". While observing the bubble chart, we can find that child with low median parent income tends to earn low income as well even the university he or she went has a high mobility rate.
